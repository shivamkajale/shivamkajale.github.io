Projects

Coherent Electrical Driving of Qubits

The idea is to exploit the fact that when an oscillating voltage is applied across a nanoscale ferromagnet, it’s magnetic moment undergoes precision. This produces an oscillating magnetic field in its vicinity which could be used to drive the qubits into desired configurations.

Performed numerical simulations based on the LLG equation describing the magnet’s dynamics and Master equation, describing qubit dynamics at room temperature. When simulated, our microwave source has been able to produce Rabi oscillations in a qubit in its vicinity

NEGF Modelling of an RTD

Modelled a resonant tunneling diode stack (Gd2O3/Ge/Gd2O3) using NEGF formulation, and simulated it on Python, and generated plots for it’s transmission coefficient and I-V characteristics for varying well width and barrier width

Proposed a design space in the well-width/barrier-width plane, based on a set of practical design constraints including switching speed, span of negative resistance region, etc.

Inorganic Perovskite Quantum Dot-based LEDs

Proposed an all-inorganic perovskite (CsPbX3) quantum dot-based LED, with a Si/SiO2 hole transporting layer (HTL) for improved carrier injection compared to ITO

Analytically computed emission wavelength of QD LEDs and verified the calculation results against experimental data on (CsPbX3) quantum dot-based LEDs in the literature. Further, used Lorentzian DoS to explain broadening of emission spectrums around peak wavelengths
